Italian Grammar Tips

Subject Pronouns

io I
tu you (informal)
lui he
lei she
noi we
voi you (plural)
loro they

Verb Endings (Present Tense)

Italian verbs change endings based on who is doing the action. Here are the patterns for -ARE, -ERE, and -IRE verbs:

-ARE (parlare = to speak)

io parlo
tu parli
lui/lei parla
noi parliamo
voi parlate
loro parlano

-ERE (leggere = to read)

io leggo
tu leggi
lui/lei legge
noi leggiamo
voi leggete
loro leggono

-IRE (dormire = to sleep)

io dormo
tu dormi
lui/lei dorme
noi dormiamo
voi dormite
loro dormono

Articles (The/A)

Definite Articles (The)

il - masculine singular (il gatto = the cat)
la - feminine singular (la casa = the house)
i - masculine plural (i gatti = the cats)
le - feminine plural (le case = the houses)

Indefinite Articles (A/An)

un - masculine (un gatto = a cat)
una - feminine (una casa = a house)

Quick Tips

Gender Matters: All Italian nouns are either masculine or feminine. Usually -o = masculine, -a = feminine.
Plural Forms: Usually -o becomes -i, and -a becomes -e (gatto gatti, casa case).
Double Consonants: They matter! "Nono" (ninth) vs "Nonno" (grandfather) - pronounce them clearly!
Formal vs Informal: Use "tu" with friends, "Lei" (capitalized) for strangers and respect.
